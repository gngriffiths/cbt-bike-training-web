The Training Day

What Is CBT & What Does It Involve?

Compulsory Basic Training (CBT) is a structured, one-day course divided into five elements. You don't need any previous riding experience — our instructors guide you every step of the way.

A
Introduction

Element A — Introduction & Safety Briefing

Your training day begins with a warm welcome from your instructor. You'll cover the legal requirements for riding, complete a straightforward eyesight check (reading a number plate from 20 metres), and receive a full rundown of the Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) needed to ride safely. Don't own gear? No problem, we provide everything.

B
On-Site Training

Element B — Practical On-Site Training

Before you ride, you'll learn how every control on the motorcycle works: throttle, clutch, brakes, and gears explained clearly. You'll practise manoeuvring the bike safely with the engine off, then learn how to start and stop the engine confidently. A relaxed, supportive session designed to build your understanding from the ground up.

C
First Ride

Element C — Practical On-Site Riding

Time to ride! In a safe, off-road environment, you'll practice moving off and stopping smoothly, changing up and down through the gears, and making controlled left and right turns. By the end of this element, most students are genuinely surprised by how natural riding begins to feel.

D
Manoeuvres

Element D — On-Site Manoeuvres & Exercises

Building on your new skills, you'll tackle more advanced exercises: figures-of-eight, controlled U-turns, riding slowly with precision, emergency stops, and riding on gradients. These exercises sharpen your control and prepare you for the unpredictability of real roads. Each exercise is practised at your own pace.

E
On the Road

Element E — Practical On-Road Riding

The final element puts everything together on real public roads. With a minimum of two hours of on-road riding, your instructor will guide you through junctions, roundabouts, and varied road conditions. All while following the Highway Code. On successful completion, you'll receive your CBT certificate, valid for two years. Then you'll be ready to ride!

Good to know: Your CBT certificate lasts two years from the date of issue. To continue riding after that, you can retake the CBT or pass your full motorcycle licence test (Module 1 & 2). Ask us about our A1, A2, and Direct Access (DAS) licence courses — we'll help you take the next step.
