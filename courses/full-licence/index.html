Direct Access Scheme

Full Motorcycle Licence

Three routes to a full licence — A1, A2, or full Category A. We'll guide you through whichever path suits your age and ambitions.

Licence Categories

Which Licence Route Is Right for You?

Your route to a full motorcycle licence depends on your age. Here's a summary of the three categories.

A1 Licence

From age 17

Take the module tests on a 125cc motorcycle. Once passed, you're licensed to ride 125cc motorcycles. The A1 is a great entry point for younger riders.

A2 Licence

From age 19

Complete the module tests on a motorcycle up to 47bhp. After passing, you can ride bikes restricted to 47bhp — including many popular 500cc machines.

Full Category A

From age 24, or 2 years after A2

Take the module tests on a motorcycle with at least 54bhp (typically 600cc+). Once you pass, you're free to ride any motorcycle of any engine size.

The Practical Test

Module One & Module Two

The practical motorcycle test is divided into two separate components. You must pass Module One before you can sit Module Two.

1

Module One — Off-Road Manoeuvres

Conducted on a spacious circuit at a local DVSA test centre. You'll demonstrate a range of skills including the U-turn, figure of eight, and an evasive swerve-and-stop exercise. Our high pass rate for Module One has earned us recognition from local test centre examiners.

2

Module Two — On-Road Assessment

A 40-minute road ride assessed by a DVSA examiner. This tests your ability to ride safely and confidently in real-world traffic conditions. Passing Module One is a prerequisite for sitting Module Two.

Before You Start

Prerequisites & What to Bring

Theory Test

You must have passed the motorcycle theory test before beginning full licence training. Book your theory test online at gov.uk or by calling 0300 200 1122.

Valid CBT Certificate

You'll need a valid CBT certificate to begin training. If yours has expired — or you don't have one — we can incorporate CBT training into the Direct Access package.

What to Bring

Your driving licence (photo card or paper + passport), appropriate clothing and footwear (helmets, jackets, gloves and waterproofs are provided), snacks or cash, and your theory and CBT certificates.
