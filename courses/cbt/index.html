Compulsory Basic Training (CBT) Brighton

The essential first step for every new motorcycle or moped rider, completed in a single day at our Brighton and Hove training site serving riders across Sussex.

About the Course

Who Is CBT For?

CBT is designed for individuals aged 16 and over — whether you're a complete beginner or looking to ride a 125cc motorcycle or scooter on UK roads.

Valid for 2 Years

Your CBT certificate is valid for two years from the date of completion. It can be renewed while still in date or on the date of expiry — if it lapses, you'll need to retake the full course.

What You Can Ride

A CBT certificate allows you to ride a 50cc scooter from age 16, or a 125cc motorcycle from age 17. You must still display L-plates and are not permitted to use motorways or carry a passenger.

Pathway to Full Licence

Completing CBT is a prerequisite for obtaining a full motorcycle licence. Our training gives you a strong foundation whether you stop at CBT or continue on to a full licence.

The Course

What the CBT Covers

Training runs from 9 a.m. (please arrive 10 minutes early to complete paperwork) and is normally completed in one day.

A

Introductory Talk

An on-site introduction covering the training plan, safety requirements, and what to expect from the day — setting you up for success before you get on the bike.

B

Practical On-Site Training

Hands-on instruction in a safe, controlled off-road environment. You'll learn to handle the machine, operate controls, and build confidence at your own pace.

C

Practical On-Site Riding

Supervised riding exercises on-site to develop your core skills — starting, stopping, turning, and manoeuvring — before progressing to the road.

D

Discussion on Road Riding

A structured debrief and discussion to prepare you for real-world riding — covering hazard awareness, road positioning, and what to expect on public roads.

E

Road Training (Minimum 2 Hours)

At least two hours of supervised riding on public roads with your instructor. No more than two riders per instructor ensures you receive focused, individual attention throughout.

On the Day

What to Bring

We provide helmets, jackets, gloves, and waterproofs — but there are a few things you'll need to bring yourself.

Driving Licence

Bring your photo card licence, or your paper licence with your passport. Without valid ID you cannot ride on the road.

Suitable Clothing

Sturdy, ankle-covering footwear and appropriate leg wear are required. Helmets, jackets, gloves, and waterproofs are all provided. See our What to Wear guide for full details.

Snacks & Refreshments

Training is a full day, so bring a snack or some cash for refreshments to keep your energy up.
