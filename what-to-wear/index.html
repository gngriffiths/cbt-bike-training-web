Safety First

What to Wear

Meeting our clothing requirements is mandatory. Here's everything you need to know before your Brighton motorcycle training course.

Important: Failing to meet our clothing requirements will result in being turned away on the day without a refund. Your safety is our absolute priority — please read this page carefully before arriving.

Provided by Us

What We Supply

The following safety clothing and equipment are included in your course price. You don't need to bring these — we've got you covered.

  • Helmet
  • Gloves
  • Jacket
  • Hi-Vis Vest
  • Waterproof Trousers

You're welcome to bring your own versions of any of the above, provided they meet the standards detailed below.

If You Bring Your Own

Clothing Requirements

If you prefer to use your own gear, it must meet the standards below. Items that don't comply cannot be used.

Helmet

Your helmet must meet at least one of the following British safety standards:

  • British Standard BS 6658:1985 and carry the BSI Kitemark
  • UNECE Regulation 22.05
  • A European Economic Area member standard offering at least the same protection as BS 6658:1985, carrying a mark equivalent to the BSI Kitemark

Leg Wear

At a minimum, wear thick jeans without any rips or tears. Damaged denim does not provide adequate protection in the event of a fall.

Footwear

Wear motorcycle boots or other sturdy boots that cover the ankles — hiking boots or Doc Martens are acceptable. The following are not permitted:

  • Steel toe cap boots
  • Trainers
  • Canvas shoes
  • High heels

Gloves & Jacket

Both gloves and jacket must be appropriate for motorcycle riding. Fashion gloves, thin fabric, or casual jackets do not meet this requirement. Purpose-built or suitably reinforced gear is expected.
